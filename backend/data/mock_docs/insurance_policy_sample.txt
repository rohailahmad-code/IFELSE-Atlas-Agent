HOMEOWNER'S INSURANCE POLICY EXCERPT - SECTION II: COVERAGES & EXCLUSIONS

SECTION II - COVERAGE A: DWELLING & PERSONAL PROPERTY (WATER DAMAGE PROVISIONS)

Subject to the terms, conditions, limits, and exclusions of this policy, we provide coverage for direct physical loss to the dwelling and personal property caused by water damage. 

1. COVERED PERILS (WATER DAMAGE)
Coverage is strictly limited to water damage resulting from the sudden and accidental discharge, overflow, or release of water or steam from within a plumbing, heating, air conditioning, or automatic fire protective sprinkler system, or from within a household appliance. Examples of covered losses include a burst copper pipe, a sudden dishwasher hose rupture, or the accidental overflow of a bathroom fixture, provided such event is abrupt, unexpected, and occurs instantly.

2. SPECIFIC LIMIT OF LIABILITY
For all covered water damage occurrences described under Section II, the maximum limit of liability for repair, replacement, or mitigation costs is capped at $50,000 per occurrence. We will not pay more than this limit for any single event, regardless of the number of rooms affected or items damaged.

3. DEDUCTIBLE PROVISIONS
A standard policyholder deductible of $1,000 shall apply to each covered loss under Section II. The deductible amount will be subtracted from the total approved claim valuation. We will pay only that part of the loss that exceeds the deductible.

4. EXPLICIT EXCLUSIONS (WATER DAMAGE)
We do not cover loss or damage caused directly or indirectly by any of the following, regardless of any other cause or event contributing concurrently or in any sequence to the loss:

A. Gradual Leaks and Seepage: We exclude coverage for loss caused by constant, repeated, or gradual leakage, seepage, or condensation of water or steam over a period of weeks, months, or years (including slow pipe leaks, hidden wall leaks, or foundation moisture). Maintenance of plumbing systems is the sole responsibility of the policyholder.
B. Flood and Surface Water: We exclude coverage for loss caused by flood, surface water, waves, tidal water, storm surge, overflow of any body of water, water back-up from sewers or drains, or water pressure below the ground surface. Protection against flood events requires a separate National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) policy.
